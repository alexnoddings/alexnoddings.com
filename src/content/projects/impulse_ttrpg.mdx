# Summary

impulse is a loose, simplified [TTRPG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tabletop_role-playing_game) framework
inspired by [improv comedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Improvisational_theatre)
and the principle of ["yes and"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yes,_and...),
where games play out like a movie that everyone is co-writing in real time.

I also designed [a website](https://alexn.dev/impulse) to explain the system and mechanics.

# Design requirements

impulse was designed to:

- Be accessible for people new to tabletop roleplaying games
- Be engaging for veteran tabletop roleplaying gamers
- Have low friction and downtime

# Core principles

- **Simplicity** - the core mechanics are incredibly simple, and should be explainable in a few short minutes
- **Maximise flow** - games should promote talking to characters, performing actions, and interacting with the world
- **Minimise ceremony** - games should reduce explaining mechanics and rules, flipping through characters sheets, and totting up dice rolls and modifiers
- **Collaboration** - above all else, impulse should be a fun time with friends!
